## 📝 The Poem

> **The Constellation of Whiskers**
>
> They are small emperors of windowsills, stitching sunbeams to sighs,  
> tail a comma that pauses the world and restarts the sky.  
> In their ears live secret maps of thunder and thyme,  
> and they fold afternoons like paper boats to send downstream.
>
> Paws like soft metronomes, they chase loose threads of light,  
> tumble through the hush between a clock tick and a dream.  
> A bell of moonlight jingles when mischief takes flight,  
> and every leap is a poem, every tumble a scheme.
>
> Curling in the hollow of a human's tired hush,  
> they purr like a weather that rolls in to mend the day.  
> Their eyes are twin lanterns that read ancient hushes,  
> turning the small, ordinary into something fey.
>
> When night unbuttons its pockets they stitch constellations on fur,  
> leave secret pawprints across the quiet of sleep.  
> Invite you to wander where ordinary things stir,  
> and plant a tiny kingdom where wonder runs deep.
